Plan Your Stay
Guided Nature Walk

We offer a guided natural walk through our Cardamom woodlands to discover the flora and fauna detailing the plantation, to unique rock formations, to small view points or to the grasslands on the edge of the forest. With prior advanced booking we can offer you a customised, one day trek through the Cardamom hills.

Bird Watching

One of the greatest blessings since we became organic is the dense population of both indigenous and migratory birds. Just by sitting around the resort itself you can spot and hear many birds like Copper Smith Barbet, Jungle Babbler, Forest Myna, Bee eaters, Red whiskered Bulbul, Malabar whistling thrush, Parakeets, Southern Caucal and many more.

Soft Trekking

We offer a daily, complimentary, inhouse group trek through our cardamom plantation. This is curated in such a manner that guests gather together for a walk in the garden breathing in the spice filled air of the plantation as a group activity. Guests can even experience picking the Cardamom themselves and learn to choose the pods carefully.

Cycling

Experience the breeze on your face as you pedal through the scenic mountainous roads of Munnar!! We offer Cycling as an activity for children around the property. For adults we offer Cycling to the nearby view point and town (this is for experienced riders who are used to tough rugged roads.)

Swimming Pool

This pool truly reminds one of the famous dialogue from the movie "Life of Pie". It is indeed for people who collect pools! It is placed beneath the Parvathy hills on the north, facing the tea plantations in the valley of chitra puram in the east and surrounded by magnificent age old trees like the Java Plums and sprinkled with spice bushes like black pepper.

Cardamom Curing House

The crown jewel of Ambady Estate, this ancient structure is the centre piece of the property which also serves as our reception. An old wood furnace from the outside heats this double heighted space in which freshly plucked cardamom is dried for 36 hours. This is the oldest method of drying this unique, fragrant spice and a tour of this attic-like space is a must.
